For this corpus and query set, baseline vector retrieval outperformed both Cohere reranking and LLM scoring — achieving Precision@5 of 0.933 compared to 0.767 and 0.633 respectively — because the queries were specific and well-aligned with the legal terminology already present in the EU AI Act and HLEG guidelines, leaving little room for reranking to improve ordering. Reranking would add the most value in scenarios where queries are ambiguous or phrased very differently from document language, where the corpus mixes more heterogeneous content types, or where the initial candidate pool contains many semantically close but legally distinct chunks; for narrow, well-indexed corpora with precise queries, metadata filtering and query rewriting are often sufficient and considerably cheaper.
