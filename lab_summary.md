Reranking helped most when the initial vector search retrieved semantically related but legally imprecise chunks, especially for EU AI Act questions involving prohibited practices, high-risk systems, and transparency obligations — cases where surface-level similarity was high but the precise legal context differed significantly between chunks. I would recommend reranking when the corpus mixes heterogeneous document types such as legal text and podcast transcripts, or when high precision matters more than latency and API cost; for narrow, homogeneous corpora where baseline retrieval already returns consistently relevant top results, metadata filtering and query rewriting are often sufficient and considerably cheaper.
